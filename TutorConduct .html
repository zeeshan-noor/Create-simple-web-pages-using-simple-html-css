TUTOR CODE OF CONDUCT

1

All tutors should respect and value each student as distinct in their own right, in their part as a member of their community and their family.

2

Respect the relationship of the Student to their siblings, parents, other fellows of their family, and other important persons.

3

Facilitate the development and optimal growth of each student to attain his or her potential. Better tutoring enables many students to transfer learning from one place to another.

4

Use information properly, maintaining confidentiality where suitable, respecting the privacy of students, respecting the right of children to be up-to-date on matters concerning themselves, and evading the misuse of personal information.

5

The tutor must be able to confess their flaws and will seek support wherever need it.

6

Respect the religious and cultural beliefs of the student and oppose any form of oppression, discrimination, or exploitation.

7

Do not comment inappropriate or suggestive remarks to or about a student, even in fun, as this could be misunderstood. Inappropriate comments include swearing, innuendo, and discussing their or your close relationships.

8

Never communicate with students via social media platforms including Facebook, Twitter, or other social media.

9

All communication with the students will be carried out using the Face2Face platform.

10

If you do wish to give the student a small reward or gift, please ensure the parents or guardians are happy for you to do so and are aware that you are doing so.

11

The tutor should understand that their role as an instructor is to help students in evolving learner independence by assisting them to learn how they best develop and learn the abilities required to accomplish their most efficient and effective learning.

12

Give accurate and honest feedback to tutees, while always demonstrating faith in their learning abilities and working to build tutees' self-confidence.

13

While tuition, pay undivided attention, and care to students.

14

Evade establishing inappropriate relationships with tutees. He or She should understand that the relationship with the student is professional rather than personal.

15

Respect every tutee's talent, skills, personal dignity, and uniqueness and at all times by accepting him or her without evaluation.

16

Not to practice a tutoring situation to preach their belief system.

17

Respect, and work to improve sensitivity to, tutees' cultural, and role issues or differences, counting those based on gender, sexual orientation, individual, age, religion, race, ethnicity, culture, disability, national origin, language, and socioeconomic level.

18

Understand the presence of individual learning styles and remain flexible in approach to tutoring.

19

Establish a safe and healthy atmosphere where tutees feel comfortable to inquire queries and admit flaws and weaknesses.

20

Ensure that the tutee has a clear recognition of the tutor's role in adding the class, not substitute for it.

21

Domain expertise knowledge and capabilities have major importance in the task as Tutor.

22

Your major incentive is building the student's self-confidence.

23

The language pupil and the instructor share must be mutually comprehensible at all times.
